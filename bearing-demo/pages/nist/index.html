NIST Cyber Security Framework Assessment

Summary

There is no Information Security Policy and the existing procedures are not robust, complete, or utilised widely within the business;

There is no Information Security Policy and the existing procedures are not robust, complete, or utilised widely within the business;

Information security roles and responsibilities are not currently defined or assigned and there is no dedicated role or team which exists to manage this function; and

There is no Disaster Recovery plan that contains a comprehensive list of consistent actions to be taken before, during and after a disaster.

Juice Shop has limited capabilities in relation identifying cyber security events other than when they are reported externally; and

The organisation is limited in their ability to monitor their network, physical environment and endpoints.

Juice Shop does not employ Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to control remote access to applications or information;

There is inconsistency in the patching of applications and operating systems; and

No monitoring service for security is currently in place.

Juice Shop is yet to implement a detailed incident response procedure;

There are no standard reports for incidents; and

Reviews that occur after a cyber-incident are not incorporated into the existing incident response procedure.
